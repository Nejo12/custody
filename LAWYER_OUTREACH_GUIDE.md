## General Family Law Firm Outreach

### Who to Target

**Ideal Law Firms:**

- **Family law specialists** (not general practice)
- **Small to mid-sized firms** (2-15 attorneys) - more agile than large firms
- **Tech-forward firms** that have modern websites and use case management software
- **Firms with multilingual services** (German + English at minimum)
- **Location:** Berlin, Hamburg, NRW (where your tool has regional data)

**Where to Find Them:**

- Rechtsanwaltskammer directories
- Google: "Familienrecht Anwalt [city]"
- Legal tech directories
- LinkedIn searches

**Red Flags (Skip These):**

- Firms that exclusively handle high-net-worth cases (‚Ç¨500+/hr rates)
- Very traditional firms with outdated websites

### Context Assessment

**What You Know:**

- He's handling your criminal case (racial discrimination/profiling)
- You've already paid him (financial relationship established)
- He doesn't know about your custody battle yet
- He's a criminal lawyer, not family law

**Strategic Considerations:**
‚úÖ **Pros:**

- Existing trust and relationship
- He already sees you as a client fighting injustice
- Criminal lawyers often know family law colleagues (can refer you)
- He may empathize with your custody situation given your discrimination case

‚ö†Ô∏è **Cons:**

### Recommended Approach with Dr. Burmeister

**Separate Conversations, Strategic Timing**

**Step 1: Mention Your Custody Battle (Next Meeting for Criminal Case)**

Don't lead with the tool. First, establish the human context.

**In-Person or Call (When discussing your criminal case):**

> "Dr. Burmeister, I wanted to update you on something personal that's affecting me during this time. Beyond the discrimination case we're working on, I'm also in a difficult custody battle for my children. It's been... mind-deteriorating, honestly.
>
> My ex is using my financial situation against me, which makes winning the criminal case even more important. I just wanted you to know, as it's weighing heavily on me right now."

**Why This Works:**

- Human connection - he sees the full picture of what you're fighting
- Contextualizes your stress/urgency
- Doesn't ask for anything, just shares
- Natural segue for him to offer help or ask questions

**His Likely Response:**

- "That's terrible, I'm sorry to hear that. Do you have a family law attorney?"
- "How is that affecting you?"
- "Is there anything I can do to help?"

**Your Response:**

- If he asks if you have representation: "Not yet - I've been navigating it myself while working on a tool to help other parents in similar situations. Actually, I built something that might interest some of your family law colleagues..."
- If he offers help: "Actually, I'd love your advice on something related. I built a legal tech tool for custody cases. Do you know any family law attorneys who might be interested in this kind of thing?"

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "This Could Replace Lawyers"

**Response:**

> "Actually, it does the opposite. We explicitly tell users: 'This tool provides education, not legal advice. You should consult a licensed attorney.' In fact, we're considering adding a 'Find a Lawyer' directory where we refer prepared clients directly to partner firms like yours."

**Data to Share:**

- LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer, etc. have INCREASED demand for lawyers, not decreased it
- Informed clients know they need help; uninformed clients think they can DIY

---

### Objection 2: "I Don't Have Time for This"

**Response:**

> "I completely understand - that's exactly why I built this. It saves YOU time. Clients do the groundwork before they ever contact you. The 15-minute demo is just to show you what your future intake process could look like."

**Offer:**

> "How about this - just give me your email, I'll send you a demo link, and you can explore it in 5 minutes when you have a coffee break. No meeting needed."

---

### Objection 3: "How Do You Make Money?"

**Response:**

> "Great question - we have a few models we're testing:
>
> 1. **Freemium:** Basic tool is free, premium PDF packages (‚Ç¨2.99-‚Ç¨29.99) with court-ready formatting
> 2. **B2B Partnerships:** Law firms can white-label this for their website as a client intake tool
> 3. **Lawyer Directory:** Verified family law attorneys can list in our 'Find a Lawyer' section for a monthly fee
>
> Right now, we're focused on feedback and usage. Monetization comes later."

**Reassurance:**

> "We're not looking for money from you - we want to understand if this is valuable. If it is, we'll build a partnership model that works for both sides."

---

### Objection 4: "I Already Have an Intake Process"

**Response:**

> "That's great - most good firms do. This isn't meant to replace your process, it's a pre-intake step. Think of it as client homework before they even contact you. They arrive already:
>
> - Understanding their options
> - Having thought through key questions
> - Knowing they need legal help (not thinking they can DIY)
>
> Your intake process becomes smoother because they're prepared."

---

### Objection 5: "Is This Legally Compliant?"

**Response:**

> "Yes - we're very careful about this:
>
> - We never provide legal advice, only legal education (citing public BGB provisions)
> - All disclaimers clearly state: 'This is not a substitute for a lawyer'
> - We don't collect sensitive data (no storage of names, addresses, etc. unless user opts in)
> - GDPR compliant - all data stored locally in user's browser, not on our servers
>
> We'd love your feedback as a legal professional on anything we should strengthen."

## Success Metrics

Track these to know if your outreach is working:

### Email Metrics

- **Open Rate:** Aim for 30%+ (family law attorneys are typically responsive)
- **Response Rate:** 10-15% is good for cold outreach
- **Meeting Conversion:** 50% of responses should lead to a demo call

### Demo Call Metrics

- **Interest Level:** Did they ask follow-up questions? (good sign)
- **Objections:** Did they raise concerns? (means they're engaged)
- **Next Steps:** Did they commit to something (referral, pilot, feedback)?

### Post-Demo Metrics

- **Referrals:** Did they introduce you to colleagues? (best outcome)
- **Pilot Partnerships:** Did they want to test it with clients?
- **Feedback Quality:** Did they give substantive legal feedback?
